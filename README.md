## 📋 DASHBOARD STRUCTURE
Built 3-page Power BI dashboard focusing on:
* Sales Overview
* Deal & SalesRep Performance
* Pipeline & Risk Diagnostic



### Page 1 – Sales Overview

This dashboard provides a high-level overview of global B2B sales performance, tracking the transition from pipeline generation to won revenue over a multi-year period (2022–2025).

#### Key KPIs:
| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Pipeline Value | € 606,037,204 |
| Won Revenue | € 240,815,432 |
| Open Deals | 6,769 |
| Avg Deal Size | € 80,299 |
| Win Rate | 60.21% |
| Avg Sales Cycle | 138 Days | 

#### Key Insights:
* Both pipeline value and won revenue show a massive upward trajectory entering 2025.
* Technology (€ 179M) and Financial Services (€ 163M) are the primary drivers of pipeline value.
* The conversion from "Lead In" (3,125) to "Contracting & Closing" (1,602) indicates a strong 51% retention through the sales stages.
* While deal volume and value are skyrocketing, the Win Rate has dipped slightly, suggesting a need to maintain lead quality during rapid scaling.



### Page 2 – Deal & SalesRep Performance

This page shifts the focus from global pipeline health to individual and team productivity, specifically drilling down into representative performance and activity metrics.

#### Key KPIs:
| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Top Performer | Eva Pereira |
| Total Sales Rep | 55 |
| Won Revenue | € 240,815,432 |
| Avg. Conversion Rate | 25.16% |

#### Key Insights:
* Eva Pereira (#1) is significantly ahead of Petra Ivanova (#2), with nearly double the won revenue (€ 16.7M vs. € 9.9M).
* Email is the dominant communication channel (72,850) compared to calls (23,500) and meetings (14,100).
* Deal performance (Open, Won, and Lost) peaked in 2025, with a visible dip projected or recorded for 2026.
* The table identifies representatives by status; for instance, Marco Popescu is marked as "Churned" yet still holds the #3 performance spot, indicating a loss of high-value talent.



### Page 3 – Pipeline & Risk Diagnostic

This page focuses on deal stagnation and risk assessment, identifying which opportunities are likely to be lost due to inactivity.

#### Key KPIs:
| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Opportunity Deals | 6,769 | 
| Healthy Deals | 1,393 | 
| Warning Deals | 1,100 | 
| At Risk Deals | 4,276 | 

[ Opportunity Deals: (Total Open), Healthy Deals: (Inactive $\le$ 30 days), Warning Deals: (Inactive > 30 & $\le$ 60 days), At Risk Deals: (Inactive > 60 days) ]

#### Key Insights:
* Approximately 63% of the pipeline (4,276 out of 6,769 deals) is "At Risk," indicating a serious lack of engagement on the majority of open opportunities.
* The "Lead In" stage has the highest volume of "At Risk" deals (over 1,000), suggesting a bottleneck in the initial qualification process.
* Integration Services and Professional Services show the highest counts of "At Risk" deals compared to other product lines.
* The "Inactive Days vs. Avg Deal Size" scatter plot shows many high-value deals are sitting silent, representing a significant potential revenue loss.

---

## 🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

* **Massive Scale:** Pipeline value and won revenue are growing exponentially year-over-year.

* **Efficiency Trade-off:** Faster sales cycles are coming at the cost of a declining win rate.

* **Severe Stagnation:** 63% of the total pipeline is "At Risk" due to long-term inactivity.

* **Performance Gap:** Revenue is heavily concentrated in a few top-performing reps (e.g., Eva Pereira).

* **Early Bottlenecks:** The "Lead In" stage holds the highest volume of stalled or risky deals.

* **Industry Drivers:** Tech and Financial Services dominate the pipeline but require better engagement.

* **Action Needed:** High-value "At Risk" deals need immediate calls to prevent revenue leakage.
